Beibei Zhang's Outlook on Interest Rates

March 29, 2025

The future of U.S. interest rates remains uncertain due to persistent inflation and the economic impact of recent tariffs. While inflation eased slightly to 2.8% in February 2025, core inflation rose, raising concerns about ongoing price pressures. Additionally, tariffs on foreign imports are fueling inflation fears and economic uncertainty. The Federal Reserve, which has kept rates at 4.25%-4.50%, had projected rate cuts in 2025, but officials are now more cautious, emphasizing the need for clearer signs of inflation cooling. Market reactions have been volatile, with concerns over stagflation growing. Ultimately, while rate cuts are still possible, their timing will depend on inflation trends and broader economic conditions.
